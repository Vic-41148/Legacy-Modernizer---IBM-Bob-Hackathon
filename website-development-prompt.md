## Phase 1: Discovery & Requirements Gathering

Before beginning the design and development process, please ask me the following clarifying questions to ensure the website meets all requirements:

### Project Foundation
1. **Project Purpose & Goals**
   - What is the primary purpose of this website? (e.g., e-commerce, portfolio, blog, corporate, SaaS, educational)
   - What are the top 3 business objectives? (e.g., generate leads, sell products, build brand awareness, provide information)
   - What problem does this website solve for your users?
   - What does success look like for this project? (specific metrics or outcomes)

2. **Target Audience**
   - Who is your primary target audience? (demographics: age, location, profession, income level)
   - Who is your secondary audience?
   - What are their pain points and needs?
   - What devices do they primarily use? (desktop, mobile, tablet)
   - What is their technical proficiency level?

3. **Industry & Competition**
   - What industry/sector does this project belong to?
   - Who are your top 3 competitors?
   - What do you like/dislike about competitor websites?
   - What makes your offering unique?
   - Are there any industry-specific compliance requirements? (HIPAA, GDPR, ADA, etc.)

### Features & Functionality
4. **Core Features Required**
   - What are the must-have features? (e.g., contact forms, search, user accounts, payment processing, booking system)
   - What are nice-to-have features?
   - Do you need e-commerce functionality? If yes, how many products?
   - Do you need user authentication/login?
   - Do you need a content management system (CMS)?
   - Do you need multi-language support?
   - Do you need integration with third-party services? (CRM, email marketing, analytics, payment gateways)

5. **Content Requirements**
   - How many pages do you envision? (approximate)
   - What types of content will you have? (text, images, videos, downloads, blog posts)
   - Do you have existing content, or does it need to be created?
   - Will content be updated frequently?
   - Do you need a blog or news section?

### Design & Branding
6. **Branding & Visual Identity**
   - Do you have existing brand guidelines? (logo, colors, fonts, style guide)
   - What is your brand personality? (professional, playful, modern, traditional, minimalist, bold)
   - What emotions should the website evoke?
   - Are there any colors you love or hate?
   - Do you have any design inspiration or reference websites?

7. **Color Scheme Preferences**
   - Primary brand color(s)?
   - Secondary/accent colors?
   - Preferred color psychology? (trust=blue, energy=red, growth=green, etc.)
   - Light or dark theme preference?
   - Any color accessibility requirements?

8. **Design Style Preferences**
   - Minimalist vs. feature-rich?
   - Modern vs. classic?
   - Flat design vs. depth/shadows?
   - Illustrations vs. photography?
   - Animation preferences? (subtle, dynamic, none)

### Technical Requirements
9. **Technical Specifications**
   - Do you have hosting preferences? (shared, VPS, cloud, specific provider)
   - Do you have a domain name?
   - What is your budget range?
   - What is your timeline/deadline?
   - Do you need a staging environment?
   - What browsers must be supported? (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, IE11)
   - What is the minimum mobile device size to support?

10. **Performance & SEO**
    - What are your page load speed expectations?
    - Do you have specific SEO goals or target keywords?
    - Do you need local SEO optimization?
    - What analytics tools do you want to use? (Google Analytics, Hotjar, etc.)
    - Do you need conversion tracking?

11. **Accessibility & Compliance**
    - What WCAG level compliance do you need? (A, AA, AAA)
    - Are there specific accessibility requirements?
    - Do you need GDPR compliance features? (cookie consent, privacy policy)
    - Any other legal/regulatory requirements?

## Phase 4: Responsive Design Strategy

### Breakpoint Strategy
- **Mobile First Approach**
  - Mobile: 320px - 767px
  - Tablet: 768px - 1023px
  - Desktop: 1024px - 1439px
  - Large Desktop: 1440px+

### Responsive Considerations
- Touch-friendly interface elements (minimum 44x44px tap targets)
- Readable font sizes across devices
- Optimized images for different screen sizes
- Hamburger menu vs. full navigation
- Stacked vs. side-by-side layouts
- Mobile-specific features (click-to-call, maps)

### Progressive Enhancement
- Core functionality works without JavaScript
- Enhanced features for modern browsers
- Graceful degradation strategy

## Phase 5: Accessibility Features (WCAG 2.1 Compliance)

### Level AA Compliance (Recommended)
- **Perceivable**
  - Alt text for all images
  - Sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 for text)
  - Resizable text up to 200%
  - No information conveyed by color alone

- **Operable**
  - Keyboard navigation support
  - Skip navigation links
  - Focus indicators
  - No keyboard traps
  - Sufficient time for interactions

- **Understandable**
  - Clear, consistent navigation
  - Descriptive labels and instructions
  - Error identification and suggestions
  - Predictable behavior

- **Robust**
  - Valid HTML/CSS
  - ARIA landmarks and labels
  - Semantic HTML structure
  - Screen reader compatibility

### Accessibility Testing Plan
- Automated testing tools (WAVE, axe)
- Manual keyboard navigation testing
- Screen reader testing (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
- Color contrast verification

1. **Clear Visual Hierarchy**
   - Most important elements are most prominent
   - Consistent spacing and alignment
   - Logical reading flow (F-pattern or Z-pattern)

2. **Intuitive Navigation**
   - Maximum 7 items in main navigation
   - Clear labels (no jargon)
   - Visible current page indicator
   - Breadcrumbs for deep sites

3. **Fast Load Times**
   - Target: < 3 seconds on 3G
   - Optimize images
   - Minimize HTTP requests
   - Use caching strategies

4. **Mobile Optimization**
   - Touch-friendly buttons (44x44px minimum)
   - Readable text without zooming (16px minimum)
   - No horizontal scrolling
   - Simplified navigation

5. **Trust Signals**
   - SSL certificate (HTTPS)
   - Privacy policy and terms
   - Contact information visible

   - Security badges (for e-commerce)

### User Flow Optimization
- Minimize steps to conversion
- Clear CTAs on every page
- Reduce form fields to essentials

#### Shared Hosting (Budget: $3-10/month)
- **Best For**: Small sites, blogs, portfolios
- **Providers**: Bluehost, SiteGround, HostGator
- **Pros**: Affordable, easy setup, managed
- **Cons**: Limited resources, slower performance

#### VPS Hosting (Budget: $20-80/month)
- **Best For**: Medium traffic sites, growing businesses
- **Providers**: DigitalOcean, Linode, Vultr
- **Pros**: More control, better performance, scalable
- **Cons**: Requires technical knowledge

#### Cloud Hosting (Budget: $10-200+/month)
- **Best For**: High traffic, enterprise, scalable apps
- **Providers**: AWS, Google Cloud, Azure
- **Pros**: Highly scalable, reliable, global CDN
- **Cons**: Complex pricing, steeper learning curve

#### Managed Hosting (Budget: $15-100+/month)
- **Best For**: WordPress sites, non-technical users
- **Providers**: WP Engine, Kinsta, Flywheel
- **Pros**: Optimized for CMS, automatic updates, support
- **Cons**: Higher cost, platform-specific

#### Static Site Hosting (Budget: $0-20/month)
- **Best For**: Static sites, JAMstack apps
- **Providers**: Netlify, Vercel, GitHub Pages
- **Pros**: Free tier available, fast, secure, CI/CD
